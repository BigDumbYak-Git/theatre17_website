  ## Irene Skene


  To read a script and just know it is the one; to get a vision of what that script would look like when brought to life on the stage; to work by the side of a remarkable woman to bring the vision to reality; to cast twelve delightful, talented, diverse women who want to share the vision; to take these women through the myriad of steps that are needed to reach production; to work with an amazing stage crew who bring the vision to life before your eyes; and then, to be able to share the vision with you, the audience?


  Marvelous!




  ## Marjorie Salavich


  Having warn a variety of hats and assisted behind the scenes with Theatre 17 for many years, when Judith suggested Irene and I should give consideration to directing, we agreed it was something worthy of checking off one's bucket list. It wasn't actually ON my list but, well ... still worth considering. It didn't take the two of us long to get started.


  Beginning with the early stages of developing the production, through meetings, auditions, casting, rehearsals, set design, and set building, it has been an enjoyable process; time consuming, but a pleasant journey. Irene and I easily slid into our preferred creative roles taking on tasks as required.


  Once we moved into The Centre for our rehearsals, set up the stage, lights, sound ... and our delicious pot luck meals - we knew we were well on our way. And the excitement was growing.


  The production crew and cast have been most supportive, and I appreciate and thank them for that. Their combined talents, strengths and weaknesses have all come into play in my 'directorial' learning curve. And special thanks to Irene. They have drawn on their own life's experiences to bring out their best for Love, Loss and What I Wore. This production sees newcomers joining the Theatre 17 repertoire of actors as well as returning members, which was very encouraging.


  I would do it again without hesitation should the opportunity arise. But perhaps I should ask them first.


  I hope you enjoy the evening. Thank you for coming.
